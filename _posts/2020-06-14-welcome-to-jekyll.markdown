This article is not about the mid 90’s Hollywood comedy film starring Jim Carey, but certainly has some connection to it.


Cometh COVID 19, the mask is reborn after 25 yrs.


It all started with the N95 mask from 3M launched to counter the pollution in Delhi NCR and has made its way down to homemade masks from worn-out clothes.

So much so that Pakistan received a consignment from China made out of used underwears. Now that’s hilarious. Used underwear 😂😂.


Well, the mask is now mandatory and is part of the official dress code. Men and women are doing masks of various colours, suiting current moods and trends.

Use n throw, washable, waterproof etc are some of the consumer choices.

Lifestyle apparel manufacturers like Wildcraft, have started a new category in their lineup.

The mask is here to stay for some time at least.


But it’s uncomfortable to wear. Seems like a foreign body, Smells awkward and eliminates one's identity.

People don’t recognise each other Even the phone fails to recognise you.

I can imagine the confusion it can create to the already chaotic official video conference calls.


After all, what is the mask intended for?

Is it to prevent the virus from infecting us or it to prevent others from getting infected- very few would know what mask to wear and when.


A commonly known fact is that the mask is mandatory and we are honest law-abiding citizens.

To implement the law, there’s a team created called “Mask-force”.

Film and sports stars are endorsing the initiative.


And then there are folks who wear the mask wrongly- by design.

Some wear it for the chin, while some wear it for just the mouth. Some are so busy that they leave it hanging from one ear.

Some wear it only when they see anyone like a cop.


Well, the mask will soon become something we would call “facial fashion” and in the process will also cripple the lipstick industry.

The French beard will become history and smoking will reduce and overall eating of nick-knacks will drop.


The mask is going to change the way we look and breathe.
